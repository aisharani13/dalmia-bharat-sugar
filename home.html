Occupational Health & Safety

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL), is a leading Indian manufacturer of speciality chemicals and
pharmaceuticals with a global footprint. With the vision to be the leader in safety, we are committed to
develop and continually improve our safety systems and culture.

We have a comprehensive Health, Safety and Environment Policy to demonstrate our commitment to
maintain the world-class standards of health and safety in line with our core values of ‘Care’, ‘Integrity’ and
‘Excellence’.

Occupational Health

We strongly believe that healthy employees are the foundation of any organisation. As ‘Care’ is one of the core values at AIL, we
are passionately committed to creating a healthy workforce. We are focused on the continual improvement of the health
standards of employees and contractors. Our commitment to community welfare and spreading awareness reflects in our
different initiatives that motivate people to improve their physical, mental and emotional well-being.

AIL has implemented guidelines on occupational health elements under Aarti Management System which incorporates best-in-
class practices related to occupational health. We have 24x7 well-equipped occupational health centres with a dedicated medical
team and state-of-the-art medical facilities, infrastructure which includes a lab for testing methaemoglobin, G6PD; audiometry,
central oxygen line, decontamination room and other emergency equipment. Ambulances with advanced facilities are available
across locations for attending emergencies. We have tie-ups with local and multi-speciality hospitals along with onboarding of
specialist doctors to ensure the best medical care for our people.

The company doctors take care of periodic as well as pre-employment medical check-ups and provide counselling to our people.
We also organise health camps and training sessions for community health awareness.

Safety

We strongly believe that for Safety, even ‘world class’ is not good enough! This belief encourages us to be better than the best in
the field of safety. AIL has adopted a cradle-to-cradle approach for driving safety in its value chain which starts from product
development to customer end.

At AIL, various leading and lagging indicators are implemented and regularly monitored through the highest level of management,
which includes safety studies, safety training, audits, TRIFR, LTIFR, LTISR and Incident of High Consequences Frequency Rate
(IHC-FR). We are certified with Integrated Management System of ISO 9001, ISO 14001 and ISO 45001.

We have established state-of-the-art process safety laboratory where various risk studies are performed with highly advanced
equipment such as Differential Scanning Calorimeter (DSC), Thermal Screening Unit (TSU), Vent Sizing (VSP-2), ARC - Accelerating
Rate Calorimeter, Flash Point apparatus and Minimum Ignition Energy to name a few.

Safe and reliable operation is assured by inherent safe design of plants. We have engaged experienced and competent people for
plant operation, and to perform safety studies and assessments such as HAZOP, HIRA, QRA, SIL, LOPA and other risk studies on a
defined periodicity for process validation.

We strive to make our workplace injury and incident free by inculcating safety culture at all levels of the organisation. We start our
day with a safety pledge to reaffirm our commitment towards safety and well-being of people. We also believe ourselves to be net
talent exporters for making the whole chemical manufacturing sector safer. We also organise various safety trainings, safety
initiatives, mock drills and campaigns inside and outside the company.

As a unique practice, we organise TACIT knowledge sessions with experienced industry professionals to enhance the knowledge
of our employees especially, the young generation.

Our infrastructure and trained man-power is equipped to handle any on-site and off-site emergency. We have mutual aid signed
with neighbouring industries and provide support to other industries and communities in case of emergency situations. We
conduct awareness sessions for nearby communities on Safety and Emergency Preparedness.

BE SAFE: Inculcating A Culture Of Responsibility

The word BE SAFE acronym to ‘By Employees Sustainability Assurance For Employees’ has turned out as a paradigm shift in the
way of life at AIL. Employees assure each other through their visible actions in the areas of Health, Safety, Environment, Fire &
Compliance. The intended goal of the BE SAFE program is ‘Assurance on Health Check of all Facilities - Zero Harm’.

To reinforce the message of safety, BE SAFE huddles are organised regularly through video conferencing. Employees across
locations participate in these huddles to share learnings from major events, success stories and their knowledge on key factors
pertaining to safety.

Development, adoption and review of guidelines and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) are major tools used for achievement
of intended safety objectives under BE SAFE Initiative. Besides, we have designed and adopted Process Initiative Common (PICs)
with specific objectives such as elimination of exposure to hazardous chemicals, reduction of electrical incidents to zero,
achieving zero leakage status, etc.

Customer Health and Safety

Customers are one of the most important stakeholders for any business. Continuing customer demand is the reason behind the
existence of businesses. Customer satisfaction helps in building loyal customer base, goodwill, market reputation and positive
word of mouth publicity. Customer centricity is one of our cultural attributes and key success factors.

Based on our high health, safety and quality standards, we have developed long-term strategic relationships with our customers
and have become ‘Global Partner of Choice’ for leading manufacturers of specialty chemicals.

We are committed to Customer Health and Safety and hence taking regular customer feedback on Health and Safety aspects of
our products. AIL supports and provides safety training to customers.

Product Safety and Stewardship

Product stewardship effort begins with a cross-functional team evaluation and prioritisation of each product or product group
according to the level of risk, determined using hazard and exposure ratings.

Product hazards are intrinsic characteristics with the potential to cause harm such as flammability, corrosivity, reactivity and
toxicity. Product exposure describes the potential contact between a product and people or the environment which is dependent
upon the type of packaging, distribution, sales volume, the number of customers and their control systems and the product
applications.

For each risk category, specific risk management practices and requirements have been identified and are reviewed on a regular
basis. We work closely with our suppliers, carriers, distributors and customers to help them maintain high safety and
environmental standards and provide them with technical assistance, training, and logistic support as well as health, safety and
environmental advice.

GPS Safety Summary

As part of our commitment to product safety and stewardship, we published the Global Product Strategy (GPS) Safety Summary
which we intended to provide the general public with an overview of product safety information about the health, safety and
environment of Chemical substance. To obtain the most accurate and current information, consult the appropriate Safety Data
Sheet (SDS) through AIL website prior to use of the material .
